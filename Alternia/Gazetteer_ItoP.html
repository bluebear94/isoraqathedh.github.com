I

Idjae Ocean

Coordinates: 0°, 15°S Indian (Ocean)

This ocean is the middle child in everything – trade volume, size, depth, biomass. It may even have the Middle Child Syndrome of oceans, if you will. It comprises a majority of the water south of the Equator and 130°W to 80°E.

Iekka islands

Coordinates: 77°W, 16°S F

A long string of volcanic islands, all inhabited and all well-known. They suck up a lot of moisture from the incoming winds, leaving Xrij just a bit drier than it could have been.

Imelle

Coordinates: 60°E, 45°N "Himalaya"

A mountain range defining the western edge of Ssiounsdu, this mountain range was created by a long-since-shut-down subduction zone. The mountains are all quite tall, but have been weathered somewhat by deep time.

J

By the design of the Alternian orthography, toponyms never start with a J in Alternia. Whoops! This section shall stay to ensure the completeness of the alphabet.

K

Katt Mountains

Coordinates: 20°W, 15°N S

A combo subduction-fold mountain range, the Katts (yes, you can call them "cats" if you so desire!) are built relatively recently, but they're not as tall as the Himalayas due to the deep angle that Sewb entered under. They do appear to be a little bit further down the subduction zone than expected, meaning perhaps the magma has been forced to bypass heavier rock.

Kapma, N. and S.

Coordinates: 74°W, 7°N "Crabman" (Nepetaquest)

A city that spreads its people across both sides of the Saesson Gap, Kapma connects its halves by building a bridge above the top. This bridge is almost 150 m above sea level below, and is generally inaccessible from the city: you have to go aorund and climb a big slope before you can cross.

Khrrup

Coordinates: 30°E, 15°N F

Khrrup is a giant triple-headed peninsula housing two sounds, the Greater Khrrup Sound and the Lesser Khrrup Sound. These large sounds makes this place a wonderful trading ground, and also a port to the distant Yiattew–Phriutch, on the far south.

Kreggle

Coordinates: 77°W, 16°S Kergulen Islands

The very last island in the Ieakka islands, Kreggle is only superficially similar to its namesake. In contrast to Kergulen, it's actually a pleasant place to live, if a bit dry.

Ksathi

Coordinates: 21°E, 11°S Skathi (Rekham)

Anoter peninsula/city combo with wonderful redwood forest and marshes. Things in Ksathi are however more vibrant than their equivalents in Tsakkta and Siggappo because there's less development in Skathi. More pristine land!

Kvumsiu River

Coordinates: 11°E, 0°N S

A fairly long but straight river that comes from Mt. Kvumsiu and drains off to Akinaks Bay. The river is lush on both banks, with thick rainforest growing on it.

Mt. Kvumsiu

Coordinates: 27°W, 10°S S

The tallest mountain on the East fencing in the Wrro Plateau. It's not very tall though – about 3000 m up – but it's enough to dry up the Plateau to become a dry land with much less biomass than it could have had.

L

By the design of the Alternian orthography, toponyms never start with a L in Alternia. Whoops! This section shall stay to ensure the completeness of the alphabet.

M

Maknos Mountains

Coordinates: 70°W, 22°S Magnos Bastil

The Maknos mountains are an ancient fold montain range that lives in Oae. The mountains are fairly weathered and usually are not very tall, and don't support a major river system. The sister Bivusti mountains in Bivu, further south, parellels the Maknos but are otherwise unrelated.

N

Natle River

Coordinates: 46°E, 30°N Nile River

The one great river saving the Great Ecchiae Desert from being completely barren, the austere Natle runs west to east down a great majority of Ecchiae to drain to the Saesson Sea. Unlike the Nile however, it has a downside: it collects nearly all the rivers heading north to drain Bakijivu, drying the land in the process.

Nakkohlep

Coordinates: 16°W, 55°S Cizin Nakolep

A deeply isolated island created by the Peetegge Spreading Ridge. There's some supergrowth on this island, but it's kept in check by the lava that sometimes spews out of the volcano. There's a weather station here, but very little else. It is however an indispensable navigational aid for Trolls and animals alike.

Nekhttae Mountains

Coordinates: 156°W, 15°S Nektan Whelan

A currently-active volcanic chain not unlike the Cascades on Earth. They're very tall and on the wrong side of the continent though – winds blowing west to East in the Southern hemisphere, they make the entire continental interior of Queewkkae a bit dry! That they are not in the horse latitudes alleviates this, but not by much.

O

Oae

Coordinates: 63°W, 17°N F

A peninsula of Ecchiae, this land surrounds the Saesson Sea, nearly touching Sewb but not quite, the resulting gap being the Saesson Gap.

Otavuw Bay

Coordinates: 30°E, 3°S Jesjae Oltaui

A long recurving bay, covered all the way by redwood forest and other similarly high-rooted trees, except where cities have taken root. In the sea are some of the largest tropical reefs in Alternia.

P

Peetegge Ocean

Coordinates: 180°, 30°N "Nepetaquest"

The largest ocean on Alternia, encompassing all water north of the Equator. It binds with the Idjae Ocean on the East side of Quuag-dulle and the Djoffa Ocean on the West side of it.

Peetegge Spreading Ridge

Coordinates: 120°E, 0° "Nepetaquest"

A long spreading ridge that starts at Yiattew–Phriutch, winds itself up its namesake the Peetegge Ocean, and then dives down to Djoffa Ocean where it loops with itself again. It's the main driver of tectonic activity in Alternia, and unlike similar spreading zones on Earth parts of it are highly visible. It's a very young Mid-Atlantic ridge, or an old African rift!

Pessa

Coordinates: 10°E, 0° Persia

The east part of Prencche, Pessa is known for having a mind of its own, both in terms of political and biological. A thin dividing ridge, no more than 450 m in height, divides the two.

Phriutch

Coordinates: 90°E, 60°S F

The larger of the two islands Yiattew–Phriutch, Phriutch is a relative hinterland, with relatively few modernization attempts and dominant wildlife. Near the centre of the land, bizarre creatures unseen elsewhere start appearing.

Prencche

Coordinates: 30°E, 0° F

A large land that leads off to Tsakkta and Ksathi, the large land is full of wonderful vegetation like redwood forests in the coasts, rainforest near the centre, and some open forests near the centre.

Prencche, Central Plateau

Coordinates: 14°E, 8°N F

A high, flat shield craton that peaks at about 1500 m, the central Prencche plateau is quite low, but that does not matter as it can catch rain quite well. It allows the proliferation of forests and redwood forests downriver.

Alternia has so many names in it! That's not surprising – it is inhabited after all. Click on a link above to go to an alphabetic listing, or continue reading to look at the syntax and details of a gazetteer entry.

Sample

The figure below shows the entry for the largest continent on Alternia, Ecchiae:

The headword, "Ecchiae" is displayed on the left side of the entry. Common nouns and non-nouns that are part of the name, such as "Great" and "Desert" in "Great Ecchiae Desert", are written after it, so "Great Ecchiae Desert" is written as "Ecchiae, Great Desert". Major features, which are mountain ranges, continents and oceans, are written in small caps.

Next come the coordinates. Only a single point is provided, even if a large location spans several. The selected coordinate is usually the one nearest to the centre, with larger lands getting rougher (less precise) coordinates. Ecchiae, above, is so large it only gets a 30°-precision coordinate.

Next come the etymology for the name. All names in Alternia are created in three ways: Class A names, in green, marked "Asia", are really Terran toponyms run over by five 18-wheelers and then reconstituted into the Alternian syllable structure. Class B names, in red, marked "Seasson", are Homestuck fanwork names run over by five 18-wheelers and then reconstituted into the Alternian syllable structure. Finally, Class C names, in blue, marked S, are names that are born out of the forge of the random name generator. In this case, S implies that the syllable is generated with simple consonant clusters, and F means that the syallable is generated with the structure's full capabilities.

Finally, there's a small blurb about the location, along with relevant links to other locations and the appropriate location in the tour.

Introduction

Before you read this article, you must remember some of these extremely important points:

PD and RD

Please note that PD and RD are not actually used as time-keeping dates in the Troll society, much like how BP is not used as a time-keeping date in our society.

"Present Day", "now"
This term refers to the time just prior to the Vast Glub, a truly colossal catastrophe where the entire race of Trolls are wiped out completely. Times after this, especially in the far future, is terribly speculative.
n PD
This syntax marks a definite date before the present day, as defined above. n is the number of Alternian years (not Earth years) that have passed. 0 PD is the date of the Vast Glub. Note, like RD below, this number is always negative.
"Recent"
This term refers to the time prior to Troll influence over the Alternian system, as defined later. Before the Recent Date, there has been no terraforming, no genetic engineering, no other anthropogenic (Trollogenic?) factors involved in the locations where Trolls are extant – no Trolls full stop, most of the time.
n RD
This syntax marks a definite date before the Recent Date, as defined above. Again, n is the number of Alternian years that has passed since the epoch of the RD. The epoch of the Recent Date, 0 RD, is defined to be −12000 PD. Again, in most cases, RD numbers are always less than 12,000, and usually RD dates are not used when RD is positive.
n thousand years ago, n ka, or n kya
These syntaxes are exactly equal to "−n RD". To reduce confusion, if used, n must be no less than 100, otherwise "−n RD" or "(−n − 12000) PD" will be used.
n million years ago, n Ma, or n Mya
n billion years ago, n Ga, n Gya, or n Bya
These syntaxes are exactly equal to "−n RD", multiplied by the appropriate factor.
"Year", "day", "radius", "diameter", "atmosphere" etc.
Unless specified, all of the above refer to Earth-based units, such as Earth year, Earth day and so on.
year, thousand years, million years, billion years
y, yr, kyr, Myr, Gyr
a, ka, Ma, Ga
Any year-related word rendered in small caps will always be with respect to Alternian years, not Earth years. All of the above refer to time intervals.

Furthermore, all numbers are accurate up to three significant figures or the nearest integer (context suffices to distinguish the two) unless noted otherwise (which includes figures such as 1.3). All numbers prefixed with "about", "around" and other such words have no significant zeros.

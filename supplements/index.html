On Circle Constants

There had been a lot of duscussion about circle constants and which ones are used. Below are the results of discussion by the various planetary parliaments, and their results.

Pseudo

Pseudo uses the ratio between the circumference of the circle and its diameter as the circle constant (C:d).

[...] while the ratio C:r gains credence due to its heavy application in advanced mathematics [...] we must not forget the cultural roots of mathematics, esentially its basis in the real world. [...] It is abundantly clear that d has more usage in practical terms, like drums and tree trunks [...]

Mathematical Resolution Regarding the "Fundamental Circle Constants", 72 308 PDN:15:4

Circular

Circular uses C:r as the circle constant.

C:r is akin to many other faculties of mathematics, esp. that of a sphere's A:r and V:r [...] usage of C:r is consistent with other units, e.g. the period of f(x) = sin x, cos x [...] that f(x) = eix has semiperiod [antiperiod] (C:r)/2 is equally fitting.

Motion to Adopt the Ratio of a Circle and its Radius to Rperesent the Ambassador of the Nonlinear Nonpolynomial-generic, 74 383 PDN:3:33

Quaxtion

Quaxtion uses the ratio of the surface area of the unit sphere to the surface area of a unit square. This is equal to four times C:d.

[...] Even as we are, we exist in a three-dimensional world. That alone is enough to at least consider the ratio A:r of a sphere. [...] By using A:r, a lot ofequations can simplify, such as F = 1/(4πε0) (Qq/r²) and ħ = h/(2π). [...] The inverse square law, indispensible to all physics, is simply understood with a proportionality constant being a multiple instead of a multiple of a multiple [...] The computational power between conversion of cricle constants is trivial[...]

Adoptation Mandate-For-Official-Publciations Regarding Circular Items, 131073.233092 EV

Xaxex

Xaxex takes the approach of adopting both C:r and C:d and instructs citizens to use them as appropriate.

[...] a five-day-long discussion amonst multiple parties, among which there are mathematicians, linguists and engineers, failed to yield any single answer. It is suggested, therefore, taht there is enough room for both circle constants to be used. The statement is "use C:d if it is simple to do so, and use C:r if it is simple to do so." [...] both constants, and their pragmatic usage, will be introduced to governmental education with immediate effect. [...]

Referendum on Circular and Spherical Constants, 4391:11:2:3

The United Front

The United Front is the general statment of all four planets. It does not endorse any circle constant.

We live [...] in a varied world, perhaps too much so for any all-encompassing standards. [...] Even in the pure world of mathematics, there are tints of culture, both intrinsically, and in its expression [...] With the above in mind, and also considering the [highly] severe computational power we have today, we [...] will explicitly disclaim any statment endorsing or disparaging the (non-)usage of any (combination) of circle constants, be it C;r, C;d, A:r, or any others. [...] No claim of any mathematical standards with Imperial scope shall have any executive effect beginning immediately.

Resolution of Dismissal of any Mathematics-related Standards with Imperial scope 40 389 262 039 AB:10:29

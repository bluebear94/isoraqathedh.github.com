The Vehicular Gender

Some parts of Egonyota Pasaru change their forms depending on gender. One might not consider this to be particularly special, but one such gender is the well-known Vehicular Gender, which is almost entirely unique amongst a large variety of languages (it isn't entirely unique of course). What had happened to the language so that it now has this strange feature? The answer is that something happened a long time ago to the civilization that hosts them, and this particular change is a trivial but useful demonstration to how culture affects language, and vice versa…

Setting

A long, long time ago, computers were invented. Then, cars were invented. Following that, digital computers were invented. The natural progression would be to then put computers onto cars. This is all the regular sequence of events that is familiar and perhaps omnipresent in all civilizations that will eventually develop into material technology (as opposed to magical technology1 or black-box technology2).

No matter which track a civilization goes down, as long as there is a need for that civilization to interact with other members of that civilization there would be a want for one in an appropriate environment to start building machines to emulate members of that civilization3 – and this happens to be so for the general Pseudoan civilization. A handful thousand years later, the closest equivalent of artificial intelligence in Pseudo was created, and it was pretty much all there.

Then… not much happened. A handful of factors caused this inaction: the proliferation of speculative literature, which embarrassingly drives a lot of legislature and political discourse in old Pseudo, has gotten many people scared enough to keep machinery and artificial minds on either side of a large tall wall, and sent many people into theoreticals rather than experimentation. The other, more approachable factor, is the fact that there really isn't any need for robots as of then – manual labour plus simple automata are easily managing the problems they then face, so there isn't so much a demand for such complex machine. Like a child with everything he wants, the civilization sits there in terms of technological development, focusing instead on other things that require more attention.

The whole idea of robots really only took off when the Pseudoans decide to go on long space journeys. Back then, when FTL wasn't (and isn't) a thing that people can do on a daily basis, trips even to nearby planets that now take mere hours can take years – Pseudoan years – and staying in a limited space for so long is just silly. Even when the idea of freezing someone into a popsicle was put forward, the problem of maintaining the ship against unpredictable factors such as fast-moving bits of debris and subatomic particles with the energy of a thrown baseball necessitated the use of things that can intelligently fix the random errors that these things make – which is robot-complex.
